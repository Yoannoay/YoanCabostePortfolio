News Sentiment Analysis

This sentiment analysis web app showcases the different perspectives presented by four international news sources, with three visualizations tracking positive and negative articles, daily changes, and polarity in topic coverage. I utilized various data processing techniques such as data extraction, cleaning, storing, analysis, and visualization to gain the following insights

  • Explore Source Code
    • This histogram illustrates the distribution of sentiment across articles from Sky News (British), Le Monde News (French), DW News (German), and CBS News (the US). Using pandas and Vader sentiment analysis, the articles were cleaned up and their sentiment scores calculated.
    This time series line chart depicts the percentage of positive articles across multiple news sources over time. Future iterations of the project will focus on optimising the database design, leveraging cloud and designing an ingestion process for increased scalability. I also hope to cover more modern hubs of information i.e. social media sites.
    This heatmap showcases the polarity of each news source's coverage of 10 pre-defined topics, which were narrowed down from 121 using spacy en_core_web_lg and similarity. The standardisation of topics was applied whilst ingesting the data into postgresql to cut down on the time the analysis took for the various visualisations. The categories were adjusted to ensure acceptable accuracy in topic assignment. Some topics were not covered by every news source in the data acquired, explaining the gaps in the heatmap. While the current topics provide an interesting overview, future iterations will focus on defining topics with more overlap to further enrich the analysis.
